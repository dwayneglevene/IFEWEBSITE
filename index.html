Institute for Entrepreneurship

Summer 2020

A five-week intensive program where students learn the principles of business entrepreneurship and software development.

Prep for Prep's Institute for Entrepreneurship (IFE) is a five-week intensive introduction to entrepreneurship and technology for rising 10th-12th graders. All students are introduced to fundamental business/marketing and technology/coding principles at the start of the program, and then divide into two concentrations for the remainder of the program: Business Plan students learn basic business and economic concepts, how to create their own business plan, and how to bring a business idea from concept to reality. Technology students learn basic front-end coding and application development. During Week 2, students are placed into startup teams and begin working together to develop and pitch tech-based businesses.

About Prep for Prep

At Prep for Prep, we have identified New York City’s most promising students of color and prepared them for success at independent schools throughout the Northeast. Once placed, we support the academic and personal growth of our students through college. Our students come from modest backgrounds and go on to excel at the most prestigious colleges and universities in the world. Many are the first in their families to graduate from college.

About Upperline Code

Upperline Code's mission is to train the next generation of computer science leaders and empower students to change their world with code. We provide computer science education programs to students, schools, non-profits and businesses around the country. Over the past five years, we have trained over 2,000 students and 200 teachers to use professional-grade coding tools and skills.
